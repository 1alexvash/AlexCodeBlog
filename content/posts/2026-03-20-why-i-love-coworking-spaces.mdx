Working remotely from home is everyone's modern dream, so I was a bit on the fence when a buddy of mine suggested trying out one of those coworking spaces.

As I just thought of it as an expensive quiet ☕️ cafe, but I couldn't be more wrong than that.

> And it feels good to be wrong! – As that's learning, so let me share what I have learned! ©️ Me

## Coworking spaces advantages:

### 1) Ergonomics

The very first advantage of coworking spaces over traditional cafes is that you always have a bigger 🖥️ desk to work on. If you are paying for the dedicates desk option, you are almost not limited at all, so you can put even three monitors on your desk or the exact amount needed for the type of work you do. Something that isn't always conveinlty possible from a cafe.

As a bonus you get highest quality chairs, best furniture, napping rooms, printers and notetakers, so you get into the perfect environment to get some work done.

Sometimes just watching some peer working with interesting setup might get you inspired to reconsider or upgrade your own working setup.

### 2) Peers

Coworking spaces are the best place to meet like-minded interesting people who also work in tech.

Since you both already work in tech, it'll be the place where you will meet one of the most interesting and like minded people.

Like for example: I met a language-learning enthusiast genius who learned five foreign languages and who was building and working on his own competitor app to kill 🦜 Duolingo. And it was the most interesting encounter of my entire 2025, and it would not have happened if I had not jumped to try to work in the coworking space.

### 3) Work life balance

The problem of working from 🏡 home is that you are never technically at "home" as you are always in the office area working. So if you post your work hours you receive some quick email to look at then you are technically working 9 or 10 hours not 8.

Coworking spaces create this strong boundary, so 🔨 work stays at work, and home becomes a place for proper leisure and rest.

So almost ironically that by going to the coworking space you will work less and even the quality of your ⌚️ work hours will increase.

### 4) Fresh air commute

In the big modern city you are usually free to pick some coworking space near your house, and there's almost some magic about this morning 🚶 commute.

Just by walking out of your building and getting to the workplace it'll immediately wake you up and set you up in the proper workstate, as when you work from home you are kind of always at home in that weird inertia, so it is harder to transition to that proper “workstate”.

### 5) Higher workrates levels

This is an individual experience, but I noticed precisely that when you work from home you never actually “rest,” so the quality of your work hour ↘️ drops and it is actually lower.

💭 Mind wandering and short attention span is very common nowadays, so at home you are much more likely to do more shallow work

### 6) Meeting space

One huge advantage of coworking spaces over cafes is that there's no loud distracting 🎙️ music, and there is always some quiet space to jump and have a meeting.

Working from a professional environment is almost like some professional, well fit  👔 shirt. It is a subtle detail but that's an edge that makes other people take you a bit more seriously and professionally.

### 7) Quietness

On a similar note, as with this one it really depends on with whom you live at home, so silence is not always guaranteed, but here coworking spaces come to the rescue again as silence is almost the core feature they offer you in the first place.

### 8) Locker

This one is like a cherry on top, but actually super useful especially when you are traveling to the new city, as you can rent coworking space for a short term without commiting to the long term real estate deal and still leave your main belongings there making you more flexible to explore the area in the city you are in 🚙 #TravelTip. 

### 9) Sense of community

I'm leaning towards more extroverted side as I love spending time with people, and I don't like being alone for a long period, so take my word with a bit of a grain of salt.

Working from home for long periods of time without any human interactions feels lonely for a long period, so I'm not huge fan of that. But having your personal coworking space you can come to is a perfect solution for that. It's not like I even need to interact with people in that WeWork like office, but it just seeing other people working around creates a very good working atmosphere and environment, and there is just something that I can't really describe, but you have to try it for yourself in that environment that just raises my mood and makes me feel better – as I think people evolved to live in tribes, so it just feels good like life is happening when people are around you, so you feel more alive.

For me a coworking space just makes my modern digital 🌳 jungles a lot more entertaining when I'm not a single person in the cave working.

### 10) Drinks & snacks

Okay, this is an interesting one. I heard many times the argument that you are almost paying for the free space you can get in that same cafe, but that's simply not correct if you do apply basic math.

First of all coworking desk space is a lot better than a typical Starbucks coffee table.

Secondly, if you multiply the price of a morning Starbucks latte that's already like 20 drinks a month you are already paying for, considering you go there every working day. If you hop in a cafe twice a day that's like already 40 drinks a month.

In a coworking space a free coffee machine is usually a basic perk included in one month's flat payment, so by replacing your Starbucks space with the WeWork like your personal office space you will already save the money for the office, and you will have so much more!

## Bottom line

Discovering coworking spaces was a big find for my personal life and my productivity levels. And if you get a chance to try it out I highly suggest it, as you might be like me very surprised by the extra edge that this unique space adds to the quality of your life.
